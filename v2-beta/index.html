PITCH WOBBLE ON MOBILE (Most common on iPhone Air) UPDATES v1.5 · since pitch-wobble notice

The Situation:
You might notice the audio briefly speed up or "pitch shift" for a second during playback. Trust me, I hear it too, and as a perfectionist, it bugs the heck out of me. After deep-diving into the code, I've confirmed the source files are 100% healthy — they play perfectly in FL Studio (that's right) and on desktop browsers.

The "Science" behind the struggle:
It comes down to the mobile device's audio stack. When the browser tries to manage dynamic BPM variations or handles background render tasks (like switching apps), the system tries to "catch up" to the buffer. It's like the device is tripping on its own feet, pre-drop paranoia from BPM changes for a split second before finding its rhythm again.

The Game Plan:
I've pinned playbackRate to 1.0, ensured the fetch is buffer-complete before playback starts, and verified no encoding anomalies in the file. The browser's currentTime still tracks accurately the whole way through, no seconds are lost which is why lock-screen scrubbing and the tuning bar stay in perfect sync even when you hear the wobble. But this specific wobble is a limitation of mobile browser architecture that's currently outside my reach as a solo dev.

Pro Tip for Pristine Gains:
If you want that 1:1 studio-quality experience without the mobile jitters, Desktop is your best bet. Or better yet, if you're a producer/DJ, pull out a real DAW or a deck and start cooking.

If you create a blend or a transition that goes hard, send it my way! I'm more of a computer guy than a producer and always stoked to see people cooking up with sick beats.

Since the last check-in, I've been putting in work to make WSI Radio faster, cleaner, and more immersive. Here's the short tour of your new gear.

THE VISUAL UPGRADE

Cleaner controls. On mobile, SCAN, PLAY, and the transport icons shrank to tidy tiles so the track name can stretch the full width of the readout. You'll always know exactly what's in the mix at a glance.

Reflection FX. Want the premium Gamer-PC aesthetic? Head to Settings → Waves → Reflection. The waveform mirrors below its horizon into a reflective pool, and the tuner panel fades to match the top-bar — the whole player reads as a single sheet of glass. Off by default; flip it on when you're in the mood.

UNDER THE HOOD

Block-loading. Instead of waiting for the whole library to load, the catalog now arrives in chunks of ten tracks. Way snappier first paint, and browsing stays smooth as the library grows. Bonus: if the main CDN ever hiccups, the player quietly falls through to a committed backup — you'll still hear something.

Rock-solid stability. Hand-tuned profiles for common mobile devices (iPhone 17 Pro in particular) so layouts look right without touching a setting. Closed a real security hole in the subtitle renderer. Disabled accidental pull-to-refresh + double-tap zoom on mobile, so you can't swipe yourself out of a song.

Browser smart-detect. If you're reading this inside Instagram, Facebook, or TikTok's built-in browser, a little nudge pointed you toward Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android). Those embedded browsers throttle audio and animation hard — switching unlocks the full, high-fidelity experience.

Harmonic Mixing (preview). Tap the KEY readout to open the Camelot Wheel. Your current key's wedge glows in the accent color while every harmonically-compatible key dims in sync — spot your next perfect mix candidate at a glance. This is a taste of what's coming in v2, so stay tuned.
